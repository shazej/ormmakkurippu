# AI Operations Pilot Plan (25-Person Firm)

## 1. Goal Restatement
The goal is to launch a 30-day AI operations pilot for a mixed technical/non-technical team of 25 to improve productivity and validate low-cost AI use cases while navigating legal and budget constraints.

## 2. Missing Information & Key Assumptions
### Missing Information
- **Specific Tools**: Whether the intention is to use general LLMs (ChatGPT/Claude), specialized coding tools (Copilot/Cursor), or workflow automation (Make.com/Zapier).
- **Core Pains**: The specific manual tasks currently slowing down the 25-person team.
- **Budget Ceiling**: The exact dollar amount for "limited budget."

### Key Assumptions (The "Pilot Baseline")
- **Scale**: All 25 employees participate in some capacity, but 5 "Champions" will lead.
- **Tools**: We will assume a "Pro" seat model (e.g., ChatGPT Team or Claude Pro) at ~$25-30/user/month.
- **Infrastructure**: The company uses a standardized cloud suite (Google Workspace or MS 365).
- **Goal**: Focused on "Administrative Efficiency" and "Creative Spark" rather than complex R&D.

---

## 3. Executive Summary
This plan outlines a **4-week phased pilot** designed to introduce Generative AI into a 25-person organization. With a focus on **low-cost, high-leverage tools**, the pilot aims to identify 3-5 repeatable use cases that save at least 2 hours per person per week. Despite the lack of current legal review, the pilot will operate under a **"Restricted Data" protocol** to ensure no sensitive client or proprietary IP is uploaded until cleared.

## 5. Risk Register (Pre-Legal Review)

| Risk | Impact | Probability | Mitigation Strategy |
| :--- | :---: | :---: | :--- |
| **Data Leakage** | High | Medium | **[STRICT]** No customer data or PII allowed. Personal data anonymization training. |
| **Hallucination** | Med | High | "Human-in-the-loop" mandatory review for all AI-generated output. |
| **Budget Overrun** | Low | Low | Prepaid monthly seats; no API-based "usage-billing" trials for now. |
| **Low Adoption** | Med | Med | Identify one "Quick Win" per department (e.g., Email drafting for Sales). |

## 7. Lightweight Training Outline (60-Minute "Jumpstart")
1.  **Fundamental Principles (10m)**: What AI is (and isn't). The concept of "Probability, not Truth."
2.  **The Perfect Prompt (15m)**: Context, Task, Format, and Constraints (C-T-F-C).
3.  **Live Case Studies (20m)**:
    - *Non-Tech*: Summarizing meeting transcripts, drafting outreach emails.
    - *Tech*: Writing unit tests, explaining legacy code functions.
4.  **The "Safety Sandbox" (15m)**: What NOT to share. How to spot hallucinations.

---

## 8. Success Metrics (First 30 Days)
- **Quantitative**: 80% seat activation (at least 3 logins/week).
- **Qualitative**: 3 "Golden Use Cases" documented (reproducible prompts).
- **Efficiency**: Self-reported time saving of >10% on routine drafting tasks.
- **Engagement**: Participation in at least 2 weekly "Prompt Share" sessions.
